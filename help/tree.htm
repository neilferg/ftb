Family Tree Diagram

Below is an extract of the BAMPOT family tree, which will be used as an example.



  • Each rectangular box is an individual, who is a member of  the tree. Males are coloured blue, females pink. For tree members, only their forenames are listed.
    • When it is available birth and death dates will also be shown. A '~' in front of a date indicates an approximation.
    • Where a birth date is available, an birth ordinal will be shown. In the diagram above, Jane Bampot is the 2nd child of Bob Bampot.
  • Each elliptical box represents a spouse (normally a marriage). The date of a marriage may be indicated by "m. date". Spouses - there may be multiple - are always placed to the right of the individual.
    In the diagram above, Elizabeth Smith is Bob Bampot's wife.
    • Married women are not treated as members of the tree - their details may be found in the tree to which is associated with their maiden name. In the example above,  Elizabeth Smith's details will be found in the SMITH tree.
  • Lines flowing down from an individual to other rectangular boxes are links to children of that individual.
    In the diagram, Dave Bampot and Jane are children of Bob Bampot.
  • Hovering the mouse over a box will usually give more information about that individual's vital record event dates.
  • If further information on an individual or spouse (vital records, census, etc) is available, it may be accessed by clicking the box. This will take you to the individual's details page.
  • Boxes that have a turn down in the top right corner indicate that there is additional information (over and above vital records) available for that individual and will be found in the "Other Notes" link for that individual. This might include things like military records, voter's roll entries, emigration etc.
