There are three headings on this page: Vital Records, Children and Secondary Records.

Vital Records

The so called vital records consist of birth, marriage and death event registration.
If a certificate is available for a particular event, it may be viewed by clicking on the certificate link

If you are interested in the process of research, clicking on the Birth:, Death: or Marriage: links (if present) will take you to to details of the searches performed.

It is possible to navigate directly to the father or mother's details page from here by clicking on their names.

Children

This section lists the individual's children. Clicking on a child's name will take you to their details page.
For a father, this will list all found children (bearing the same surname).
For a mother, however only those children whose birth record explicitly states her to be the mother will be listed. This limitation arises from the fact that the majority of people were found though census searches, which give limited information on the parentage of a child.

Secondary Records

  • Census Searches. These are a very important source of information on a family. Census are held every 10 years in the UK. The earliest is 1841, and the latest is 1911.
  • Other Notes This is a sort of catch all, but may include things like military records, emigration, voter's roll entries etc.
  • Other Files This is a list of miscellaneous files that are not referenced in any other document pages.
